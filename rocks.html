Earth Science

1. Structure of the Earth

Layers

  • Crust: Outermost, thin layer (Continental ~35 km, Oceanic ~7 km).
  • Mantle: Thick, semi-solid, extends to 2900 km, convection currents move plates.
  • Core: Outer core (liquid), Inner core (solid, very hot).

Discontinuities

  • Mohorovičić (Moho): Boundary between crust & mantle.
  • Gutenberg: Boundary between mantle & outer core (S-waves disappear).
  • Lehmann: Boundary between outer core & inner core.

2. Rocks & Minerals

What are Minerals?

Minerals are natural, inorganic substances with a definite chemical composition and crystal structure.

  • Examples: Quartz, Feldspar, Mica

Types of Rocks

  • Igneous: Formed from cooled magma/lava (Granite, Basalt).
  • Sedimentary: Formed from sediments, may contain fossils (Limestone, Sandstone).
  • Metamorphic: Changed by heat & pressure (Marble, Slate, Gneiss).

Rock Cycle

Magma → Igneous → Sedimentary → Metamorphic → Magma

3. Plate Tectonics

Types of Plate Boundaries

Divergent (Constructive)

  • Plates move apart
  • New crust forms (Mid-Atlantic Ridge)
  • Sea-floor spreading, rift valleys

Convergent (Destructive)

  • Plates move toward each other
  • Types:
    • Oceanic–Continental → Volcanoes (Andes)
    • Oceanic–Oceanic → Island arcs (Japan)
    • Continental–Continental → Fold mountains (Himalayas)

Transform (Conservative)

  • Plates slide past each other
  • No crust created or destroyed
  • Example: San Andreas Fault

4. Earthquakes, Volcanoes & Hotspots

Earthquakes

  • Sudden release of energy along faults.
  • Mostly occur at plate boundaries.
  • Measured using:
    • Richter scale: Magnitude
    • Mercalli scale: Intensity

Volcanoes

  • Openings where magma reaches the surface.
  • Found at divergent & convergent boundaries, and hotspots.
  • Types: Shield, Composite, Cinder Cone

Hotspots

  • Strong heat plumes from deep mantle.
  • Not related to plate boundaries.
  • Examples: Hawaii, Yellowstone
